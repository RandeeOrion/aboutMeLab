Welcome!

My name is Randee and while I spent my whole rural childhood desperately waiting to leave for the city, and I spent my young adulthood as an urban dweller, as a proud middle aged woman, I am now dreaming of returning to my rural roots.

Much to the dismay of my Bellevue raised husband, our front yard is a gloriously, unruly and muddy in-ground garden, complete with five equally unruly chickens: Rey, Commander Leia, Darth Vader, Chewbacca, and Rosabelle. We have volunteer tomatoes plants commandeering space in the very intentionally painted cut flower patch, sunflowers peeking out of the beans, weeds we pretend are ground cover and as you walk around do be careful to look out for the glorious chicken poop that feeds it all.

Every year I dig up more sod, collect more free raspberry canes from the side of the road, sprinkle clover seeds in the grass that is left in the hopes the bees will be better fed, and attempt to add hedges of witches hazel and elderberry. When I am finished with the Code Fellows program I plan on building a bigger coop, brood some fluffy ducklings and fill out my flock to the full eight the city of Seattle allows. If the timing is right I also plan on adding two colonies of honey bees.

My plan is to continue to grow our urban farm until may husband finds the situation so ridiculous that he relents and agrees to buy several acres on Vashon Island and move. At which point, I can finally invent in the dairy cow I have been dreaming of, a small herd of alpacas, goats and sheep, and a sheep dog, learn how to herd and compete in the Sheep Dog Classic on Vashon.
